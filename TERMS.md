Oligolia's *Export for Synthesis* feature generates an order file on your
computer in the format a DNA synthesis vendor's ordering portal expects. If
enabled, using this feature would require agreeing to the following.

## What this feature does — and does not — do

- **It generates a file only.** Oligolia writes the order file to a location
  you choose. It does **not** submit, transmit, or place any order with any
  vendor, and it does **not** contact a vendor on your behalf.
- **You submit the order yourself** by uploading the generated file on the
  vendor's own website, under that vendor's terms and pricing.
- **Oligolia does not verify** the correctness, manufacturability, legality,
  or safety of any sequence you export. That review is entirely your
  responsibility before you place an order.
- Export formats are based on each vendor's published ordering
  documentation where available; where a vendor's column layout hasn't been
  confirmed against its own current published spec (see `verified=False` in
  `backend/formats/synthesis_order.py`), that's disclosed at export time and
  you must cross-check the generated file against the vendor's current
  template before uploading.

## Research use only

Oligolia is a design and analysis aid for research purposes — not intended,
validated, or authorized for clinical, diagnostic, therapeutic, or any
other medical use. This mirrors the standard "RUO" designation used
industry-wide by DNA synthesis vendors (e.g. IDT: *"For research use only.
Not for use in diagnostic procedures."*).

## Your responsibilities

- **Review every sequence before ordering.** Confirm it is correct and is
  the sequence you intend to synthesize.
- **Comply with biosecurity screening and applicable law.** Do not use this
  feature to design or order sequences derived from Select Agents /
  regulated pathogens or toxins, or anything otherwise unlawful in your
  jurisdiction. Reputable vendors screen orders; that screening does not
  remove your own legal and ethical obligations. Comply with your
  institution's biosafety committee (IBC) requirements before wet-lab work.
- **You are responsible for all costs** incurred with the vendor.

## Third-party data sources

Oligolia queries NCBI, Ensembl, UniProt, KEGG, Reactome, and STRING.
Oligolia does not control, and cannot guarantee the accuracy, completeness,
or availability of, these external sources.

for orders placed, costs incurred, or consequences arising from sequences

## Privacy

Oligolia is designed to run offline and does not collect telemetry. It
transmits data only when you take an explicit action that does so (e.g.
searching a public database, or exporting/uploading a synthesis order
yourself).
